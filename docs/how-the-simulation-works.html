01

Genesis — building a galaxy

Everything starts from a single integer. genGalaxy(seed) seeds a mulberry32 RNG and lays down a whole world before the clock ever starts. Same seed in, identical galaxy out — the simulation is fully deterministic.

seed A number becomes a random stream

makeRng(seed) turns one integer into a deterministic sequence. Every roll after this — a star's position, a war's outcome — is drawn from it, so replaying a seed replays history exactly.

worldgen 96 systems in 6 clusters

Systems scatter around six cultural cluster-centers, each with fertility, mineral & energy reserves, habitability, a culture vector and a founding faith. Then jumpgates wire each to its 2 nearest neighbors, a union-find pass forces one connected graph, and a few long trade lanes are struck across the gaps.

seeding Life, powers & merchants

The 40 most livable worlds get starting populations. 12 founding factions claim well-spaced high-population capitals; 5 merchant houses charter fleets at the richest ports; and 4 creeds take root in the old cultures.

burn-in 300 silent years

Before you ever watch, the engine runs 300 years headless to settle the galaxy into a living, uneven state — realms formed, lanes worn in, some worlds already dead. Then the clock hands over to real time.

02

The heartbeat — simulateYear(w)

One tick advances the galaxy one year. It runs eight phases in a fixed order, and order is load-bearing: each phase mutates the one shared world object, and later phases read what earlier ones just wrote. Tap a phase to see what it touches.

year++ then snapshot alive = systems with pop > 0.05 ↺ then the whole cycle repeats next year
why order The alive list is snapshotted once, before economy runs, and shared by every phase. Economy sets prices and well-being → Trade moves goods along those prices → Settlement reacts to well-being → Politics taxes the new wealth → and so on. Reorder the phases and you get a different galaxy.

04

Why it holds together

Nothing keeps the galaxy alive on purpose. It stays interesting because the phases form closed feedback loops — every excess creates the pressure that corrects it. There is no rubber-banding and no safety rail.

The market finds its own price

scarcity→ price→ labor shares→ production→ stock↺

Prices come from local scarcity; labor reallocates toward whatever pays (with inertia), which lifts supply and drags the price back down. Hungry worlds shift hard into farming on their own.

Trade erases the gaps that feed it

price gap> freight + duty→ goods flow→ prices converge↺

Houses haul goods across a gate only when the price difference beats freight and tariffs. Each shipment narrows the very gap it chased — and better gates make more lanes worth running.

Empires overextend and break

annex→ admin ∝ size^1.35> tax income→ stability↓→ secede / collapse

Bigger realms cost superlinearly more to govern, and distance from the capital compounds it. Grow past what the treasury bears and the fringe secedes — the empire prunes itself.

Well-being moves people

food + goods→ well-being→ migrate / colonize→ pop redistributes↺

Well-being is how fed and supplied a world is, capped by habitat. Low → people emigrate to better neighbors; high & crowded → they found colonies. Famine (below 45% fed) culls population directly.

War is geography

culture + creed rift→ rivalry→ war→ gate battles → sieges→ exhaustion → peace

Rivalry grows from cultural distance, shared borders and rival faiths — and falls with mutual trade. Wars are fought gate by gate; sieges starve worlds until they fall, then bankruptcy forces peace.

Culture & faith drift with the freight

busy lane→ cultures converge· creed spreads big→small↺

Heavily-trafficked lanes pull two worlds' cultures together and carry the larger world's religion to the smaller. Isolation does the opposite — cultures drift apart and cut-off worlds schism into heresies.

◆ the whole point

Every loop is a negative feedback dressed as a story: surplus invites trade that kills the surplus, conquest invites the cost that ends the conquest, prosperity breeds the crowding that scatters it. The galaxy never settles — it oscillates, and the Chronicle just names the swings: Golden Lanes, the Withering, the Burning Years.

05

Engine & presentation, cleanly split

Everything under src/sim/ is pure, DOM-free JavaScript that mutates the world and nothing else. The React layer is a read-only viewer on top — which means the same engine runs headless in Node.

src/sim/ — the engine

No DOM, no framework. New mechanics slot in as another phase, shock or stat without touching a line of UI. It can be exercised entirely from the command line.

# deterministic headless run
npm run sim               # seed 42, 500 yrs
npm run sim -- 12345 2000 # custom seed + years

src/ui/ — the viewer

React reads the world each tick and paints it: a canvas map of territories and convoys, a scrubbable timeline, a live event ticker, and side panels for systems, powers, trade and the chronicle.

# watch a galaxy live
npm run dev               # dev server
npm run build             # build to dist/
